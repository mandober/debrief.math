# Reciprocals

Pairs of trig functions are each other's reciprocals
- sin and csc
- cos and sec
- tan and cot

`[f⁻¹(x)]⁻¹ = f(x)` where `f = {sin, cos, tan, csc, sec, cot}`
- [sin⁻¹(x)]⁻¹ = sin(x)

```js
            1                     1
sin(x) = ------        csc(x) = ------
         csc(x)                 sin(x)

            1                     1
cos(x) = ------        sec(x) = ------
         sec(x)                 cos(x)

            1                     1
tan(x) = ------        cot(x) = ------
         cot(x)                 tan(x)
```

- reciprocal of `f(x)` is `f⁻¹(x)`
- inverse of `f(x)` is `-f(x)`; 
- actually the inverse of `y = sin(x)` is `x = sin(y)`
